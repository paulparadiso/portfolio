New York Historical Society Luce Center

Three interactive touchscreen experiences built as part of a permanent installation at the museum. The first presents visitors with an interactive timeline showing the history of Manhattans grid with a series of videos and first hand documentation as well as an interactive maps that compare Manhattans shape over time. The second presents a series of objects that are thought to represent the 21st century that visitors can learn as well as vote on and suggest new ones. The third is a history of graffiti in New York that allows visitors to view videos and read a few texts on the topic.

Flight

Created with Marina Zurkow. An installation that presents a more complete view of the atmosphere surrounding several airports. It uses real-time data of airport arrivals and departures, weather reports, bird and ufo sightings and satellite positions as input for the animated environment.

Raw War

An Installation to accompany the film Women, Art, Revolution by Lynn Hershman Leeson. The installation consisted of a database of underrepresented artwork by women that users could explore using wiiMotes disguised as flashlights. The project also included a website where the artwork could be viewed and where users could upload work that they felt should be included. The installation first exhibited as part of Sundance New Frontiers coinciding with the premier of the film.

YouPrison

A project designed by Dillee Scofidio + Renfro meant to imagine possible prison environments. The installation consisted of an LCD touchscreen mounted to a pole in the center of a room. The LCD could be swiveled 360 around the pole creating a window into a virtual prison the users interact with to control certain parameters of the space.
